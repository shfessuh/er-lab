Structural Drivers

A county-level indicator framework for characterizing the multidimensional structural drivers of MCH and their role in racial/ethnic health disparities across the United States.

What are structural drivers?

Structural drivers are the policies, institutions, and systems that generate inequalities in socioeconomic status and health—they are the "cause of the causes." This is a critical distinction from social determinants of health (SDOH). Where established SDOH measures like the Area Deprivation Index aggregate individual Census data to characterize area-level deprivation (e.g., proportion of homeowners), structural driver indicators aim to capture the institutional processes that produce those outcomes (e.g., mortgage lending practices, zoning policy). This distinction matters for intervention: identifying structural processes points toward upstream policy targets with greater population health impact than interventions focused on modifying individual behaviors or social conditions.

Why these indicators and data?

Each domain captures a distinct mechanism through which structural conditions generate inequities in social determinants and health outcomes. Indicators were selected to measure the process rather than the outcome, prioritizing publicly available, county-level data with near-universal coverage across the ~3,143 U.S. counties

About

About

This project was developed to support research that seeks to understand how structural drivers—policies, systems, and institutional conditions—shape population health and health inequities across place, with a particular focus on maternal and child health. Rather than relying solely on traditional social determinants of health measures which aggregate individual-level data, our project centers upstream structural factors such as housing and lending systems, labor markets, education, infrastructure, environmental exposures, and political and economic conditions.

The data ecosystem harmonizes data from multiple public sources, integrating them at the U.S. county level and documenting them for research use. By providing cleaned, linked, and well-documented data across domains, the project lowers barriers to place-based, equity-focused research, supports causal and spatial analyses, and informs policies and interventions aimed at addressing the root causes of health inequities.

Datasets

This framework groups key structural drivers into datasets that help explain how racial and ethnic disparities in maternal and child health emerge and persist. Each domain contains publicly available datasets that measure conditions at the county level across the United States. These datasets reflect long-term patterns in exposure, access to resources, and public and private investment.

Environment

These datasources capture the physical conditions of place that shape health through two mechanisms: toxic exposures (air quality, traffic, contaminated water, toxic release sites) that have direct biological pathways to adverse health outcomes, and infrastructure access (transit, broadband, energy burden, land use) that determines whether residents can reach employment, care, and services.

Code Subdomain Dataset Description
en1 Pollutants EPA National Emissions Inventory This dataset contains the National Emissions Inventory (NEI) from the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency, which provides comprehensive estimates of air emissions for criteria pollutants, precursors, and hazardous air pollutants across the United States. The NEI is compiled every three years using data from state, local, and tribal agencies and EPA models and supports air quality and environmental exposure research.
en2 Traffic NANDA Traffic Volume This dataset provides estimates of roadway traffic volume aggregated to ZIP Code Tabulation Areas (ZCTAs), compiled by the National Neighborhood Data Archive (NaNDA). The data capture spatial variation in transportation intensity and are used to assess mobility patterns and traffic-related environmental exposures.
en3 Toxic Release Sites EPA Toxic Release Inventory Sites This dataset contains facility-level data from the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency’s Toxic Release Inventory (TRI), which tracks annual releases and transfers of toxic chemicals to air, water, and land. Facilities across multiple industrial sectors report emissions, enabling analysis of environmental hazards and regulatory exposure.
en4 Water Safe Drinking Water Inventory System This dataset provides regulatory and compliance information on U.S. public drinking water systems from the EPA’s Safe Drinking Water Information System. Data include system characteristics, monitoring results, and violations, supporting analyses of drinking water quality and access.
en5 Internet Access NaNDA from Federal Communications Commission This dataset provides small-area estimates of household internet access by census tract and ZIP Code Tabulation Area. The data capture geographic disparities in digital connectivity and broadband access.
en6 Land Use NaNDA National Land Cover Database This dataset provides census tract–level measures of land cover and land use characteristics compiled by NaNDA. Variables describe the physical and environmental composition of neighborhoods and support analyses of urban form and environmental context.
en7 Energy Expenses Low Income Energy Affordability Data This dataset documents state and local energy planning policies compiled by the National League of Cities. It includes information on energy initiatives and planning efforts relevant to local governance and sustainability.
en8 Transit NaNDA from National Transit Map This dataset includes counts and locations of public transit stops aggregated to the census tract level. It supports analyses of transit accessibility and neighborhood transportation infrastructure.

Housing

These data sources measures the institutional systems that determine where people live and under what conditions, capturing the structural processes that produce segregation and housing insecurity. Indicators include residential segregation indices, mortgage lending practices, foreclosures, subsidized housing supply, and rental market conditions.

These datasets capture structurally distinct mechanisms: school segregation reflecting policy decisions about district boundaries and attendance zones, school access, and school resource allocation reflecting how property-tax financing creates a structural feedback loop between housing markets and educational opportunity. Together these indicators measure the quality and equity of educational infrastructure counties produce

Work

These datasets measures the structure of local labor markets industry composition, job growth, wage levels, and union density . These indicators capture whether the jobs available in a county come with economic security, health benefits, and worker protections, and whether workers have collective bargaining power, all of which determine the material conditions that mediate structural drivers and health outcomes.

Code Subdomain Dataset Description
w1 Job Oppurtunities US Census County Business Patterns This dataset provides county-level data on business establishments, employment, and industry composition. It supports analyses of local economic structure and labor markets.
w3 Job Oppurtunities BLS Quarterly Census of Employment and Wages This dataset from the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics’ Quarterly Census of Employment and Wages (QCEW) provides county-level data on employment, establishments, and wages, supporting analysis of local labor market structure and economic conditions.
w4 Unions Office of Labor Management Standards Online Public Disclosure This dataset contains union financial disclosures and governance records from the U.S. Department of Labor’s Office of Labor-Management Standards. It supports analyses of labor organization activity and transparency.

Power

These datasets measure the structural mechanisms through which communities can or cannot influence the policies that govern them and the degree to which they are subject to punitive state systems. Voter participation and political contributions capture civic and financial political power; incarceration rates capture how mass criminalization extracts individuals from communities, generating chronic stress and economic instability that are documented drivers of health disparities.

Code Subdomain Dataset Description
p1 Voting NaNDA from Federal Election Assistance Comissions This dataset provides county-level measures of voter registration, turnout, and partisan composition. It supports analyses of political participation and electoral context.
p2 Incarceration Vera Institute Incarceration Trends This dataset provides historical data on incarceration rates and prison populations compiled by the Vera Institute of Justice. It supports analyses of criminal legal system trends over time.
p4 Political Contributions Open Secrets Data
