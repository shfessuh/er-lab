About

Racial and ethnic disparities in maternal and child health are driven by upstream structural conditions such as housing, education, labor markets, infrastructure, and governance.

Domains

This framework groups key structural drivers into domains that help explain how racial and ethnic disparities in maternal and child health emerge and persist. Each domain contains publicly available datasets that measure conditions at the county level across the United States. These datasets reflect long-term patterns in exposure, access to resources, and public and private investment.

Environment

Environment Variables

This data collection focuses on environmental conditions that shape exposure and access across U.S. counties. It includes publicly available datasets on pollutants, traffic volume, toxic release sites, drinking water systems, land use, energy costs, internet infrastructure, and public transit. These indicators reflect how environmental risks and resources are distributed and how they contribute to place-based disparities in health and well-being.
